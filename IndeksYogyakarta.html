Project information

  • Category: Remote Sensing
  • Short Title: Mapping the Relationship Between NDVI, NDBI, and LST: Exploring Vegetation, Urban Areas, and Surface Temperature in Yogyakarta (2019–2024).
  • Affiliation: Personal Project

Description:

Through spatial analysis, I’ve created a series of maps using Landsat-8 satellite imagery to visualize the relationship between NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index), NDBI (Normalized Difference Built-up Index), and LST (Land Surface Temperature) in Yogyakarta over two time periods: 2019 and 2024. Key Insights:

1. Vegetation-rich areas (high NDVI) show significantly lower surface temperatures (low LST), emphasizing the cooling effects of green spaces.

2. Built-up areas (high NDBI) correlate with increased surface temperatures (high LST), clearly illustrating the Urban Heat Island effect.

3. Over the five-year span, changes in these indices highlight the impact of urbanization and land-use changes on surface temperature dynamics.

This time-series analysis not only provides critical data to understand environmental changes in Yogyakarta but also supports ecosystem rehabilitation strategies and informed urban planning.
