Project information

  • Category: Remote Sensing
  • Short Title: Maps of Pollutant Concentrations (SO₂, CO, NO₂) and Monthly Trends in Kutai Kartanegara, East Kalimantan (2023).
  • Affiliation: Personal Project

Description:

In this research project, I conducted an extensive analysis of air pollutant distribution patterns in Kutai Kartanegara, East Kalimantan, using Sentinel-5P satellite imagery data throughout 2023. The study focused on three major atmospheric pollutants: Carbon Monoxide (CO), Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂), and Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂), revealing significant spatial and temporal variations across the region. The temporal analysis demonstrated notable patterns, with CO concentrations reaching a peak of approximately 9,750 μg/m³ in October 2023, following a dramatic increase from its lowest point of 6,750 μg/m³ in August. This pattern suggests a strong correlation with seasonal activities in the region. NO₂ concentrations exhibited two distinct peaks during the year – one in April (5.25 μg/m³) and another in October-November (5.45 μg/m³), with consistent low periods during July-August (3.5 μg/m³). The SO₂ measurements showed more frequent fluctuations, featuring a significant spike to 116 μg/m³ in June 2023, while maintaining a baseline of approximately 95 μg/m³ throughout the year.

The spatial distribution analysis revealed that CO concentrations (ranging from 6,554.4 to 8,991.3 μg/m³) were predominantly higher in the southern region of Kutai Kartanegara, while NO₂ levels (0.16-10.44 μg/m³) showed notable concentrations in urban and industrial areas. SO₂ measurements (40.7-204.1 μg/m³) exhibited distinct hotspots in the southeastern region. These patterns strongly correlate with documented land-use changes in East Kalimantan during 2023, as reported by Global Forest Watch, which recorded approximately 89,000 hectares of primary forest loss in the region. Additionally, Forest Watch Indonesia's reports indicate a 12% expansion in mining operations during the same period, contributing significantly to the observed pollutant concentrations.

This comprehensive analysis utilized advanced remote sensing techniques and GIS mapping to process the Sentinel-5P data, providing crucial insights into the environmental impact of rapid industrial development and deforestation in the region. The observed temporal patterns, particularly the consistent upward trends in both CO and NO₂ baseline concentrations, suggest a gradual deterioration of air quality throughout the year. These findings serve as valuable indicators for environmental monitoring and policy development in Kutai Kartanegara, offering a scientific basis for future environmental management strategies and highlighting the need for balanced development approaches in this ecologically significant region. Environmental Context The observed pollutant patterns correlate with documented land-use changes in East Kalimantan during 2023. According to Global Forest Watch, the region experienced approximately 89,000 hectares of primary forest loss in 2023, with significant portions in Kutai Kartanegara (Global Forest Watch, 2023). Forest Watch Indonesia reports that mining operations in East Kalimantan expanded by 12% in 2023, contributing to increased industrial emissions (FWI Annual Report, 2023). Significance The temporal analysis reveals clear seasonal patterns in pollutant concentrations, with notable peaks during the dry season (August-October). These patterns align with increased industrial activity and seasonal agricultural practices in the region. The consistent upward trends in both CO and NO₂ baseline concentrations suggest a gradual deterioration of air quality throughout the year.
