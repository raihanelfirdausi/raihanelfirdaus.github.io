Project information

  • Category: GIS
  • Short Title: Bali - Island of the Gods
  • Affiliate: Personal Project

Description:

This contour map presents a comprehensive visualization of Bali's topography, showcasing elevation variations from lowlands to mountain peaks. Created at a scale of 1:550,000, this map integrates data from Rupa Bumi Indonesia (RBI) with Digital Elevation Model (DEM) processed through the Google Earth Engine (GEE) platform. The visualization employs an informative color gradient to represent 14 elevation classes, ranging from 0 to 3,100 meters above sea level.

The prominent topographical features highlighted in this map include Mount Agung, reaching 3,031 meters above sea level, and Mount Batur at 1,717 meters, both serving as dominant landmarks that shape Bali's landscape characteristics. The detailed contours displayed not only reveal elevation variations but also reflect the natural terracing patterns that have become characteristic of Balinese agriculture and culture.

This map offers various practical applications, from geomorphological analysis and land-use planning to climatological studies and tourism sector development. The detailed topographical data presented makes this map a valuable reference for academic purposes, research, and development planning in Bali. The combination of RBI data and DEM processing through GEE has resulted in an accurate and informative visualization, providing deep insights into the topographical characteristics of the Island of the Gods.
