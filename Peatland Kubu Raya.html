Project information

  • Category: Remote Sensing & GIS
  • Short Title: Peatland Monitoring and CO₂ Emission Calculation in Kubu Raya Regency, West Kalimantan
  • Affiliation: Personal Project

Description:

This study presents a comprehensive analysis of land-use changes and associated CO₂ emissions in the peatland areas of Kubu Raya Regency, West Kalimantan, Indonesia, from 2019 to 2023. The investigation employed Sentinel-2 satellite imagery, utilizing a supervised classification approach with the Random Forest algorithm to categorize land cover into five distinct classes: water bodies, infrastructure, shrubland, cropland, and forest. To enhance classification accuracy and monitor temporal changes in vegetation health, soil exposure, and water content, the study incorporated three spectral indices: the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI), Soil Adjusted Vegetation Index (SAVI), and Normalized Difference Water Index (NDWI). The inclusion of SAVI particularly strengthened the analysis by minimizing soil brightness influences on vegetation monitoring.

The research methodology encompassed the development of a transition matrix to quantify land cover changes between 2019 and 2023, revealing significant land-use transitions, particularly from forested areas to alternative land uses. CO₂ emission calculations were derived from emission factors established by the National Forest Reference Level for Deforestation, Forest Degradation and Enhancement of Forest Carbon Stock, as specified in UNFCCC Decision 12/CP.17 paragraph 12. The analysis quantified substantial CO₂ emissions from various forest conversion categories: forest to infrastructure conversion resulted in emissions of 83.48 tonnes CO₂, forest to shrubland transition generated 227,500.10 tonnes CO₂, and forest to cropland transformation produced the highest emissions at 852,551.71 tonnes CO₂. The total CO₂ emissions demonstrated a significant increase from 4,276,833.15 tonnes in 2019 to 4,591,020.46 tonnes in 2023, underscoring the considerable environmental impact of land-use changes in the region.

The spatial analysis and visualization were conducted using QGIS software, generating a comprehensive suite of cartographic outputs. These include temporal spectral indices maps illustrating NDVI, SAVI, and NDWI variations, land cover classification maps for 2019 and 2023, emission factor distribution maps, land-use change transition matrices, and comparative CO₂ emission analyses. This research provides essential insights into the environmental implications of land-use changes in Kubu Raya's peatlands, offering valuable data to support evidence-based environmental management strategies and inform regional policy decisions.
